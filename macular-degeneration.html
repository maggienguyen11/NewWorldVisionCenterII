Macular Degeneration

Macular degeneration is caused by the deterioration of the critical central portion of the retina, the inside posterior layer of the eye that records the images we see and then sends them via the retina to the brain. The macula, helps to focus central vision, and controls our ability to read, drive a car, recognize faces or perceive colors, and see objects in fine detail.

Tips to prevent Macular Degeneration

  • Don't Smoke as Beta Carotene, Carotenoids, and Lycopens can increase Lung Cancer Risks
  • Eat Plenty of Fruits and Vegetables
  • Take Multivitamins
  • Consider an AREDS Nutritional Supplement OcuVite and ICaps
  • Eating More Fish
  • Exercise Regularly to Maintain Optimal Weight
  • Eat Whole Nuts Almonds Cashew and Peanuts
  • Reduce Refined Carbs and avoid High Fructose Corn Syrup in Your Diet
  • Control Blood Pressure and Lower Cholesterol
  • Wear Sunglasses With UV & Blue Light Protection
  • Have Regular Professional Vision Exams
